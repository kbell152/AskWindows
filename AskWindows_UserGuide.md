# Ask Windows — User Guide

Ask Windows is a helper app for your Windows PC. You can type questions or speak
them out loud, and Ask Windows answers in plain language — no jargon, no guessing.

---

## Getting started

When you open Ask Windows for the first time, you'll see a short setup screen.
It asks for:

- **Your invite code** — the code you were given to use the app. Type it carefully
  and click Continue.
- **Your name** — so Ask Windows can address you personally.
- **How comfortable you are with computers** — this helps Ask Windows pitch its
  answers at the right level.
- **How you'd like it to talk to you** — friendly, warm, or casual.
- **How you click and scroll** — touchpad or mouse, so instructions match how
  you actually work.
- **Your Windows version** — Ask Windows detects this automatically and shows it
  to you. Just confirm it's correct.

Once you click Continue, you'll go straight to the chat screen and Ask Windows
is ready to use.

---

## Asking a question

Type your question in the box at the bottom and press Enter, or click the blue
arrow button ➤. Ask Windows will think for a moment and then answer.

You can ask things like:

- "How do I make the text on my screen bigger?"
- "Where do I find my downloaded files?"
- "How do I connect to my Wi-Fi?"
- "What's the quickest way to take a screenshot?"
- "How is Windows different from Mac?"

Ask Windows remembers what you've said earlier in the same session, so you can
follow up naturally — "Can you show me a different way?" or "What did you just
say about screenshots?"

## Using your voice

### Speak a single question

Click the **microphone button** 🎤 near the bottom right. Speak your question.
When you're done, click the button again (it turns into a stop button ⏹). Your
words will appear in the text box — press Enter or click ➤ to send them.

### Conversation mode (hands-free)

Click the **waveform button** 〜 (to the left of the microphone). Ask Windows
will start listening. Just speak your question naturally. After you stop talking
for about 4 seconds, Ask Windows sends your question automatically, thinks about
it, and reads the answer aloud. When it finishes reading, it starts listening
again — ready for your next question.

A small label shows you what's happening: **Listening…**, **Thinking…**, or
**Speaking…**

To stop conversation mode, click the waveform button again.

If you want to pause the microphone temporarily during conversation mode, click
the microphone button. It will show a muted mic icon. Click it again to resume.

---

## Hearing answers read aloud

Every answer from Ask Windows has a small **speaker button** 🔊 on the left.
Click it to have Ask Windows read the answer aloud. Click it again to stop.

Ask Windows uses the best voice available on your PC. If you've downloaded a
better voice in Windows Settings → Time & Language → Speech, it will use that
automatically.

---

## Making the text bigger or smaller

- **Ctrl and +** (hold Ctrl, press the plus key): make text bigger
- **Ctrl and –** (hold Ctrl, press the minus key): make text smaller
- **Ctrl and 0** (hold Ctrl, press zero): reset to normal size

The size is remembered the next time you open the app.

---

## Changing your settings

Click the **gear icon** ⚙ in the top right corner to open Settings. You can
change your name, skill level, and tone at any time. If you need to enter a new
invite code, click "Start over" — this clears your details and takes you back to
the setup screen.

---

## What Ask Windows can and can't help with

Ask Windows is set up to help with your **Windows PC** — things like:

- Using Windows and its built-in apps (Edge, File Explorer, Settings, Photos,
  Notepad, and so on)
- Switching from Mac to Windows
- Everyday tasks like printing, taking screenshots, connecting to Wi-Fi, or
  adjusting settings
- Installing and using popular apps like Chrome, Zoom, Spotify, or Microsoft Word

If you ask about something outside this area, Ask Windows will let you know
that's outside what it reliably helps with, and point you somewhere that can.

## If something doesn't look right

**Ask Windows says it can't hear you or the microphone isn't working:**
Open Windows Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone, and make sure
microphone access is switched on. Also check that AskWindows is allowed in the
list of apps.

**The answer seems wrong or the steps don't match what you see on screen:**
Tell Ask Windows what you actually see — describe it. Ask Windows will adjust
based on what you report.

**Windows shows a security warning when you open the app:**
Click **More info**, then **Run anyway**. You only need to do this once.

**Ask Windows says it can't reach the server:**
Check that your PC is connected to the internet and try again.

---

## A note on privacy

Ask Windows sends your questions (and your name, skill level, and Windows
version) to a private server to get answers. Nothing else is shared. Your voice
is processed by a speech recognition service and is not stored or used for
anything else.
